Drain Current Under Repeated Presynaptic Spikes
A train of 1 V, 200 Hz presynaptic pulses is applied with varying ON/OFF timing (Fig. 6). Each spike leaves behind un-relaxed protons, so consecutive peaks climb toward a saturating final value — shorter OFF windows leave less time to relax, producing the strongest potentiation.

CH6 · CONDUCTANCE MODULATIONVg = 1 V
Potentiation ↔ Depression Cycle
Repeated gate pulses raise channel conductance (potentiation) as protons accumulate; once pulsing stops, slow relaxation of the electric double layer lets conductance decay back down (depression) — the device's analogue of a biological synapse strengthening and forgetting (Fig. 8).
